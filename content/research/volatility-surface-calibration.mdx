## Overview

The implied volatility surface encodes the market's consensus view of future
variance across strikes and maturities. Fitting a smooth, arbitrage-free surface
to a finite set of observed option prices is a prerequisite for delta-hedging,
risk management, and exotic pricing.

This project benchmarks three industry-standard parameterizations:

| Model | Parameters | Arbitrage-free? | Speed |
|-------|-----------|----------------|-------|
| SVI (Gatheral 2004) | 5 per slice | With constraints | Fast |
| SABR (Hagan et al. 2002) | 4 | Approximately | Very fast |
| Dupire local vol | Continuous | By construction | Slow |

## Data

We use end-of-day SPX option chains from [Data Source], spanning 12 months of
trading days in 2024. After filtering for liquidity (bid–ask spread < 5% of
mid, open interest > 100), the cleaned dataset contains approximately 180,000
observations across 40+ expiries per day.

## Key Findings

- **SVI** achieves the best out-of-sample fit (RMSE in implied vol terms)
  when calibrated with the calendar-spread no-arbitrage constraints from
  Gatheral & Jacquier (2014).
- **SABR** is substantially faster but produces arbitrage violations near
  the wings for short-dated options.
- Local volatility surfaces exhibit well-known forward smile flattening that
  missprices cliquets and forward-starting options.

## Methodology Note

All calibrations use sequential quadratic programming (SQP) initialized from
the previous day's parameters, which significantly reduces convergence time
and improves stability.
