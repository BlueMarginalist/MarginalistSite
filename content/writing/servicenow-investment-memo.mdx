*Current Price: $91 | Target Price: $152 | Rating: BUY | May 14, 2026*

## Summary

I think ServiceNow is a Strong Buy at $91, with a base-case target price of $152, implying 67% upside. The stock has declined approximately 28% year-to-date amid a broad software sector selloff driven by fears that AI will disrupt seat-based SaaS business models.

The market is mispricing ServiceNow for two reasons: (1) the company is diversifying away from seat-dependent revenue, with 50% of FY25 NNACV already from seat-independent sources — AI-driven upsell, cross-sell, and token-pricing would effectively monetize on seat-reduction; and (2) ServiceNow holds a differentiated position to monetize the emerging AI orchestration layer due to its single codebase, unified data model, and 22 years of proprietary operational workflow data.

At the current price, the market assigns little probability to ServiceNow sustaining 20%+ growth or winning a meaningful share of the AI orchestration opportunity. Our analysis suggests this is a mispricing. Even in the bear case (target: $87), downside is limited, while the base and bull cases ($152 and $212) offer asymmetric upside.

## The SaaS Selloff and Bear Thesis

2026 has been a brutal year for software stocks. The sector selloff was triggered by fears that AI builder tools could substitute established enterprise software, and that AI-driven labor market effects would reduce the total number of seats enterprises need.

Recent client interviews conducted by UBS and JPM seem to confirm the bear thesis: most clients interviewed report a slowdown in the hiring process and a reduction in non-AI IT budget. Few report a specific cut in license spending, but more say it may decline as a result of slowed hiring and seat cuts.

ServiceNow has declined approximately 28% YTD, broadly in line with peers such as Salesforce (−24%) and Workday (−25%), but significantly underperforming hyperscalers like Google (+108%) and Microsoft (−14%). The market is sharply differentiating between companies perceived as AI beneficiaries (infrastructure providers) and those seen as AI disruption targets (horizontal SaaS).

Among those deemed AI losers, an argument proposed by Workday's CEO Aneel Bhusri attacks ServiceNow further: companies serving as systems of record would be less likely to be replaced, as they essentially serve as a database that transfers governance and reporting risks. Basic enough that AI cannot automate the process, yet mission-critical enough that companies would not try to vibe-code a vulnerable alternative.

## Consensus View vs. Our View

**The consensus holds that:** seat reduction is destructive for future growth and, without a 20%+ CAGR story, ServiceNow cannot justify its premium valuation; its workflow automation exposure makes it more vulnerable to substitution than system-of-record vendors; and the stock's premium over Salesforce (25x vs. 14x forward P/E) may not be warranted if growth decelerates.

**We disagree on three points:** ServiceNow's platform embeddedness is sticky — its single codebase presents a higher probability of monetizing the emerging AI orchestration layer than any competitor; Now Assist growth and cross-sell data suggest the company can partially offset seat reduction by upselling existing customers and shifting to token pricing; and at the current price, the market is pricing near-zero probability of orchestration success and permanent growth deceleration, creating an asymmetric risk-reward.

## Company Overview

Headquartered in Santa Clara, ServiceNow is the dominant enterprise workflow automation platform — the software infrastructure that IT, HR, security, and customer service teams use to manage and resolve work across the organization. It is the only major enterprise platform built on a single codebase with a unified data model, meaning every module shares the same database, audit trail, and workflow engine, giving its AI agents native access to operational context that fragmented competitors cannot replicate.

ServiceNow generates 97% of revenue from subscriptions, with a 98%+ net retention rate. The company has expanded from its ITSM core into HR service delivery, customer service management, security operations, low-code development (App Engine), and AI-powered automation (Now Assist). In FY2025, the company served 8,400+ enterprise customers including 85%+ of the Fortune 500, with 603 customers at $5M+ ACV.

## Thesis #1: The Seat Reduction Bear Case Is Overstated

### Diversifying away from seat-dependent revenue

The market narrative assumes ServiceNow is a pure-play ITSM company whose revenue is heavily exposed to per-seat pricing compression. This is no longer accurate. Key evidence:

- 50% of FY2025 NNACV came from non-seat-based revenue sources.
- Now Assist AI launched Q4 2023 and reached $750M ACV by Q1 2026 in just 8 quarters, with a raised 2026 target of $1.5B. Management guides AI to represent 30%+ of total ACV by 2030.
- Data and Analytics: 2× NNACV year-over-year growth, on a 25%+ CAGR trajectory.
- 91% of FY2025 NNACV came from deals with 5+ products, validating the platform strategy.

### AI-driven upsell and cross-sell offsets seat compression

ServiceNow's tiered pricing structure (Standard, Pro, Plus) creates a natural upsell path as AI adoption accelerates. Now Assist features are only accessible to Pro and Plus subscribers, prompting migration from Standard to higher-value tiers. The pricing uplift is substantial: Standard to Pro represents a 25% price increase; Standard to Plus represents a 60% increase.

Our upsell model estimates that tier migration adds approximately 4 percentage points of annual growth on top of a 14% base volume growth rate, taking the effective CAGR from 12% (volume only, net of 2% churn) to approximately 16%. By 2030, Standard tier share of the installed base declines from 45% to approximately 21%, with the average revenue index rising from 1.14× to 1.23× Standard-equivalent.

### AI inference cost is manageable

A key investor concern is that AI inference costs will erode gross margins. The evidence suggests this risk is bounded:

- Management states AI reasoning accounts for less than 10% of cost to serve, and Now Assist gross margins remain above 80%.
- Non-GAAP subscription gross margin has compressed approximately 200 basis points (from 84.5% to 82.5%), but this is partially attributable to Armis intangible amortization rather than inference costs.
- ServiceNow generated $300M in annualized AI-driven cost savings ($100M in 2025, $200M in 2026 at flat headcount), roughly offsetting the gross margin drag.
- Industry inference costs are declining 30%+ annually (NVIDIA H100 pricing fell 64–75% from Q4 2024 to Q1 2026), which structurally improves the margin trajectory over time.
- ServiceNow prices on outcomes (resolved tickets, automated workflows), not tokens, giving it pricing power above marginal inference cost.

Even assuming Now Assist gross margins of 65% — below the 80%+ management claim — token-based revenue per seat is approximately 8× traditional seat revenue, leaving ample buffer for margin downside.

## Thesis #2: Upside From the AI Orchestration Layer

### A decentralized, not winner-take-all, outcome

The emerging consensus from enterprise CIOs is that the AI orchestration layer will be decentralized. Just as no Fortune 500 company runs HR, CRM, ITSM, and communications under a single vendor today, the AI central brain will similarly be distributed across specialized platforms. UBS client interviews confirm this, with F50 and F100 executives describing a plug-and-play system where each SaaS vendor connects as an outlet within a broader orchestration framework.

In this model, each vendor's competitive moat rests on its proprietary data and business logic. ServiceNow holds 10+ years of CMDB, ITSM, and operational workflow data; Workday holds HR data; Salesforce holds CRM data. An agent built by ServiceNow cannot perform as well on the Salesforce system, and vice versa. Even neutral vendors like Microsoft, building a universal orchestration layer, cannot match ServiceNow's ITSM performance without calling ServiceNow's API. Everyone captures some portion of the agent monetization opportunity. The question is how much.

### ServiceNow's structural advantages

**First, the single codebase and unified data model.** ServiceNow is the only major enterprise platform where every module (ITSM, HR, CRM, Security) shares the same underlying table structure, audit trail, and workflow engine. Competitors like Salesforce grew through acquisition (MuleSoft, Tableau, Slack, Informatica) and stitch together siloed data models. Microsoft spans more enterprise domains but across at least four disconnected product families. ServiceNow's native integration means an AI agent can traverse IT, security, HR, and customer data in a single query without cross-system API calls — reducing latency, hallucination risk, and governance complexity.

**Second, the security and governance position.** AI agents will not only read data but also write and delete, making security oversight critical. Non-security SaaS platforms (Workday, Salesforce) have limited risk and security exposure, while security pure-plays (CrowdStrike, Palo Alto) lack workflow orchestration capabilities. ServiceNow sits uniquely at the intersection — with its AI Control Tower, the Armis and Veza acquisitions, and a Security segment growing at 40%+ NNACV year-over-year. One F50 executive in UBS interviews noted they would consider the AI Control Tower because nothing else comparable is available.

### Assessment

Combining the decentralized nature of the future agent ecosystem with ServiceNow's favorable positioning in data quality, platform breadth, and security governance, we believe the company holds a slight edge in monetizing the orchestration opportunity. Supporting factors include a strong execution track record (consistently beating guidance), a COO recruited from Google Cloud, and an expanded Microsoft partnership. Critically, the stock is currently priced with little-to-no probability assigned to the orchestration thesis, meaning any positive signal represents upside optionality.

## Valuation

### Comparable companies

ServiceNow trades at 18.9× FY+2 forward P/E and 14.7× NTM EV/EBITDA — a premium over Salesforce (11.6×), Workday (10.0×), and Adobe (9.4×), but one that has compressed significantly from historical levels. ServiceNow's trailing 3-year average P/E was 110×; the current 54× trailing P/E is a historical low. The premium reflects a genuine growth differential: 21% organic revenue growth versus low-teens for Salesforce and single digits for Adobe, or equivalently, a Rule of 60+ versus Rule of 30 and below for peers.

### DCF analysis

Our DCF is anchored on FY2027 free cash flow of $5.04 per share (consensus). Three scenarios:

- **Base Case ($152):** ~20% revenue CAGR driven by CRM and Creator segment growth at 25% CAGR (below management guidance of 35%+), flat Technology Workflow growth supported by upsell and limited seat compression, and Now Assist ACV reaching $1.5B in 2026. Terminal multiple of 30× FCF (18× EV/EBITDA), reflecting the return of a modest premium over Microsoft as AI disruption fears fade. Exit multiple method yields $168; perpetuity growth method yields $130. We anchor on $152.

- **Bull Case ($212):** Higher terminal multiples of 20× EV/EBITDA reflecting meaningful AI orchestration layer monetization, with higher CRM and Creator segment growth. Revenue reaches $34.3B by 2030, above management's $30B target.

- **Bear Case ($87):** Growth decelerates to a 17.3% five-year CAGR, driven by lower-than-expected CRM and Creator growth, with the terminal multiple compressed to 13× EV/EBITDA. The orchestration thesis fails to materialize and ServiceNow reverts to a decelerating enterprise SaaS grower.

At the current price of $91, the market is pricing roughly the bear case, assigning minimal probability to sustained 20%+ growth or orchestration success. We view this as a mispricing.

## Risks

**Idiosyncratic:** Enterprise CEOs need time to assess ROI on AI bundles, and seat-reduction effects may materialize before token-based upsell fully offsets. If new agent protocols (MCP, A2A) enable robust cross-system execution, ServiceNow's platform advantage erodes.

**Industry and macro:** Geopolitical risk overhang may reduce enterprise IT budgets over the next 6–12 months, affecting the ability to upsell. If AI agents become more robust at handling data quality issues and cross-system execution natively, that favors general-purpose platforms like Microsoft over specialized workflow vendors.

## Catalysts

- Continued Now Assist ACV growth toward the $1.5B 2026 target, validating AI monetization at scale.
- Organic cRPO growth maintaining 20%+ levels after M&A integration effects (Moveworks, Armis) fade.
- Positive customer signals from the AI Control Tower product, converting pilot deployments into contracted revenue.
- Q2 2026 earnings demonstrating that the Q1 Middle East and federal headwinds were transitory, not structural.
